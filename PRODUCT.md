# Product

## Register

product

## Users

One owner reviews their own Apple Health and imported workout history on a desktop, usually to understand training consistency, aerobic development, recovery, and recent performance without sharing the data with anyone else.

## Product Purpose

Turn private fitness records into clear, honest training analytics. The app should make longitudinal patterns easy to see, preserve the detail behind each conclusion, and distinguish measured facts from estimates. It is informational fitness software, not a medical product.

## Brand Personality

Precise, restrained, and candid. The interface should feel like a serious personal instrument: calm at rest, dense when useful, and never promotional.

## Anti-references

- Social fitness feeds, leaderboards, engagement loops, and public comparison.
- Generic wellness dashboards built from interchangeable pastel cards.
- Metrics presented with false precision or sports-science claims unsupported by the stored data.
- Decorative color, gradients, shadows, and novelty that compete with the data.

## Design Principles

1. Show the evidence behind the summary.
2. Prefer honest unavailable states to invented precision.
3. Make long-term history as useful as the latest sync.
4. Keep navigation and interaction quiet so measured data carries the emphasis.
5. Reuse familiar structures across modalities while letting each sport surface its own meaningful metrics.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Support keyboard navigation, visible focus, semantic labels, dark and light themes, reduced-motion preferences, and meaning that never depends on color alone. Desktop density is appropriate, but text and controls must remain legible when the window narrows.
